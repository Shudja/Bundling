This example displays four different options. For each option, each urn was created before the start of the experiment according to their creation rules. For the urn in Option A, the computer flipped a computerized coin that had an equal chance of landing on heads or tails. If the coin landed on heads, then the urn in Option A has 89 green balls and 11 purple balls inside of it. If the coin landed on tails, then the urn in Option A has 11 green balls and 89 purple balls inside of it. For the urn in Option B, the computer flipped another, separate, computerized coin that had an equal chance of landing on heads or tails. If the coin landed on heads, then the urn in Option B has 63 green balls and 37 purple balls inside of it. If the coin landed on tails, then the urn in Option B has 37 green balls and 63 purple balls inside of it. The urn in Option D is also created using a computerized coin. Notice that the urn in Option C has a 100% chance of having 45 green balls, so you know it has 45 green balls and 55 purple balls.

After reading each urn's creation rule, you would then make two decisions in this example Situation regarding these four options. You will first choose one of these four options. After making this decision, you will then be shown the color of the ball drawn from the option's urn and you will be paid for it if it is a green ball. This ball will then be placed back in its appropriate urn. You would then again choose one of the four options.
