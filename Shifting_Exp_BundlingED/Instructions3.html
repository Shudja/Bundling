In each Situation, you will make decisions involving four options. Each option contains two urns, which each have {{ C.NUM_BALLS }} balls inside of them (which can either be green or purple). Each urn comes with a description of how it was created (i.e., an Urn Creation Rule). For example, the first urn in the first option might have a 50% chance of having 72 green balls and 28 purple balls and a 50% chance of having 33 green balls and 67 purple balls. The other urns are similar to this urn but have their own creation rules. For example, the second urn in the third option might have a 50% chance of having 50 green balls and 50 purple balls and a 50% chance of having 39 green balls and 61 purple balls. The first urn in the fourth option might have a 100% chance of having 80 green balls and 20 purple balls.

In each Situation, you will be presented with four options that each have two urns. The urns used in a Situation were created independently from urns in other Situations. That means the creation of an urn is unaffected by the creation of any other urn. If the Urn Creation Rule is the same in two different Situations, the urns themselves are new in the sense that a separate instance of the Urn Creation Rule generated the urn in each Situation. In each of these Situations, you will make two choices between the four options. Whenever you choose an option, you will draw a ball from each of the two urns in the option. Whenever you draw from an urn and the drawn ball is a green ball, you will receive {{ C.BET_PAY }} points. Whenever you draw from an urn and the drawn ball is a purple ball, you will receive 0 points.

In each Situation, you will first decide which option to implement. You will then be shown the color of the ball drawn from each urn in the option and you will be paid for any green balls that you draw. These balls will then be placed back into their appropriate urns and you will make a second decision between the four options. You will once again decide which of the four options to choose. You will then be shown the color of the ball drawn from each urn and you will be paid for any green balls that you draw. You can choose the same option for both choices, or different options, it is up to you.

As mentioned above, the urns presented to you in a Situation are new urns that are separate from the urns in the other Situations. These urns do not change within a Situation. That is, these urns were created at the start of the experiment according to their Urn Creation Rule and any ball drawn is placed back inside the appropriate urn before the next decision. Once again, we do not observe the composition of any of these urns until the end of the experiment.
